# Bird Powers: Flocking

This document details the various bird powers related to flocking, which involves tucking cards under a bird. Each tucked card is worth 1 point at the end of the game.

**General Rule:** Birds with this power are known for gathering in large single-species flocks. As described on these cards, you can build a flock of a certain species by tucking cards face-down under that bird card. Each tucked card is worth 1 point at the end of the game.

| Bird(s) | Power | Trigger | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Horned Lark | When another player plays a grassland bird, tuck a card from your hand behind this bird. | `ONCE BETWEEN TURNS` | This is specifically referring to birds played into the grassland habitat. |
| Red-winged Blackbird, Common Grackle, Brewer's Blackbird, Yellow-headed Blackbird, Bushtit, Dickcissel | Tuck a card from your hand behind this bird. If you do, also lay 1 egg on this bird. | `WHEN ACTIVATED` | A combination of tucking and egg-laying. |
| American Coot, American Robin, Barn Swallow, House Finch, Purple Martin, Ring-billed Gull, Tree Swallow, Violet-green Swallow, Yellow-rumped Warbler | Tuck a card from your hand behind this bird. If you do, draw 1 card. | `WHEN ACTIVATED` | You can only gain the benefit if you first tuck a card from your hand. |
| Cedar Waxwing, Dark-eyed Junco, Pine Siskin, Pygmy Nuthatch | Tuck a card from your hand behind this bird. If you do, gain a [specific food] from the supply. | `WHEN ACTIVATED` | A combination of tucking and gaining food. |
| American White Pelican, Black-bellied Whistling-Duck, Canada Goose, Double-crested Cormorant, Sandhill Crane | Discard 1 [specific food] to tuck 2 cards from the deck behind this bird. | `WHEN ACTIVATED` | You must have the food in your personal supply; you cannot take it from a bird card. You must discard the food to take 2 cards from the deck and tuck them behind the bird. |
